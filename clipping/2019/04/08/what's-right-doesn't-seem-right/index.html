What's Right Doesn't Seem Right

Leadership is so hard to get right because what's right doesn't seem right. For example:

To be viewed as credible, we have to admit things that might erode credibility (e.g. mistakes).

To help our team solve problems, we have to avoid stepping in to solve the problem ourselves.

— Claire Lew (@clairejlew) February 21, 2019

This tweet reminds me of so many things:

  1. The Fourth Law of Trust from The Secrets of Consulting

    The trick of earning trust is to avoid all tricks.

  2. Romer's Rule from The Secrets of Consulting

    The best way to lose something is to struggle to keep it.

  3. Why Greatness Cannot Be Planned: The Myth of the Objective

    The path to success is through not trying to succeed.

I guess that's why life's so hard.
